AI just made MRI 30% faster.
So why will your radiologist shortage get worse?

A new npj Digital Medicine study validated AI denoising across 148,930 real-world MRI pairs - cutting acquisition time by 30% on average, some scans under 1 minute, diagnostic quality intact.

That's the Jevons Paradox at work in radiology.

Named after economist William Jevons: when a resource becomes more efficient, total consumption rises - not falls. Cheaper to use = used more.

Faster scans  more scans ordered per session -> More scans -> more findings flagged -> More findings -> more radiologist time, not less

The capacity gain on the scanner side doesn't translate to capacity gain on the reporting side. It just moves the bottleneck.

The practices winning with AI aren't the ones with faster scanners. They're the ones that solved the reporting workflow first.
